TIYANAK

     Tiyanak or impakto are babies who died before receiving baptism rites. After death, they go to a place known as Limbo, a chamber of Hell which unbaptized dead people fall into, and are transformed into evil spirits. These phantasms return into the mortal realm in the form of goblins to eat living victims.

Appearance

     While various legends have slightly different versions of the tiyanak folklore, the stories all agree on its ability to mimic an infant, with its ability to imitate an infant's cries for luring victims.[3] In some legends, the Tiyanak may take the form of a specific child.

Origin

     The true origin of Tiyanak came from the Malaysian creature, Pontianak or Mantianak - a female viscera (just like an aswang). They share many similarities, but the Pontianak is the mother who died while pregnant, Tianak is the ghost of the child, not the mother herself. But on my understanding, both Langsuir and Pontianak perhaps the mother of the Philippine Tianak, because both died in connection with giving birth or while in pregnancy.

Countermeasures

     In local belief, various countermeasures are supposedly effective against the tiyanak. Those that were led astray by the creature's cries are believed to be able to break the enchantment by turning their clothes inside out. The tiyanak finds the method humorous enough to let go of the traveler and go back to the jungles. Loud noises such as a New Year's celebration are also thought to be enough to drive the tiyanak away from the vicinity.[3] Objects believed to repel Aswang (vampires), like garlic and the rosary, are also commonly believed to be effective against the tiyanak.
